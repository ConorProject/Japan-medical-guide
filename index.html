🚫 Prohibited Medications

These medications are strictly prohibited from entering Japan and can result in arrest:

Stimulants & ADHD Medications

Adderall, Ritalin, Vyvanse, Dexedrine, and any amphetamine-based medications

Pseudoephedrine

Sudafed, Actifed, and cold medicines containing pseudoephedrine

Codeine

Tylenol with Codeine, prescription cough syrups, and codeine-based painkillers

Cannabis Products

CBD oil, medical marijuana, and any cannabis-derived products (even legal ones)

📋 Medications Requiring Permission

These require advance approval from Japanese authorities:

Opioid Painkillers

Morphine, Oxycodone, Fentanyl patches - requires Narcotics Import Certificate

Insulin & Diabetes Medications

More than 1 month supply requires Import Certificate for Personal Use

Psychotropic Medications

Antidepressants, antipsychotics in quantities over 1 month supply

Heart & Blood Pressure Medications

Large quantities may require documentation

📞 How to Get Permission

For Narcotics: Contact the Regional Bureau of Health and Welfare at your entry point in Japan, at least 2 weeks before travel.

For Other Medications: Apply for "Import Certificate for Personal Use" through the Pharmaceuticals and Medical Devices Agency (PMDA).

Required Documents: Doctor's prescription, medical certificate explaining need, copy of passport.

Processing Time: 2-4 weeks. Apply well in advance of travel.

Emergency Contact: Contact the Japanese Embassy/Consulate in your country for urgent cases.
