📋 Step-by-Step Permit Application Process

Step 1: Determine If You Need a Permit (Day 1)

  • Use the search tool above to check your medication status
  • If "Permit Required", proceed to Step 2
  • If "Prohibited", consult your doctor about alternatives BEFORE travel

Step 2: Gather Required Documents (Days 2-5)

From Your Doctor:
  • Medical certificate stating diagnosis & necessity
  • Original prescription with your name
  • Treatment plan/dosage schedule
You Prepare:
  • Copy of passport
  • Travel itinerary
  • Completed application forms

Step 3: Initial Email Inquiry (Day 5-7) - OPTIONAL but Recommended

⚡ You have two options:

A) Send email first, wait for guidance (safer but slower)
B) Skip email, go directly to online application (faster but riskier)

To: yakkan@mhlw.go.jp
Subject: Medication Import Inquiry - [Your Name] - [Travel Date]

Dear MHLW,

I am traveling to Japan on [date] and need to bring [medication name].
- Diagnosis: [your condition]
- Medication: [generic name], [dose] x [quantity]
- Duration: [X days supply]
- Entry airport: [Narita/Haneda/Kansai]

Please confirm the correct application process and required documents.

Thank you,
[Your name]
✅ Advantages of Email First:
  • Confirms you're using correct process
  • May get specific guidance for your medication
  • Creates paper trail
  • Can clarify document requirements
⏰ If Time is Short:
  • Can skip to online application
  • Email response takes 2-5 business days
  • Most standard medications don't need special guidance
  • Risk: might need to resubmit if wrong

Step 4: Submit Online Application (Day 8-14, or immediately if skipping email)

📌 When to Submit Online Application:

• If you sent email: Wait for reply (2-5 days), then submit based on their guidance

• If skipping email: Submit immediately with your best judgment

• If no email reply after 5 days: Proceed with online submission anyway

1

Access portal: impconf.mhlw.go.jp

2

Create account with passport details

3

Select medication type and complete forms

4

Upload all documents as PDFs

5

Submit and note application number

6

Check email daily for updates or document requests

⚠️ Important: The online system may ask for additional documents after submission. Respond within 24-48 hours to avoid delays. This is why starting early is crucial!

Step 5: Follow Up & Receive Certificate (Days 14-28)

  • Check email daily for questions or approval
  • Respond to any requests within 24 hours
  • Print permit certificate when received
  • Make 3 copies: customs, carry-on, checked bag

📝 Customs Declaration Form - What to Declare?

🎯 Quick Answer: Do I Need to Declare My Medication?

YES, ALWAYS DECLARE if:
  • It's a psychotropic drug (Ritalin, Concerta, Xanax, etc.)
  • It's a narcotic painkiller (codeine, morphine, oxycodone)
  • You're unsure about its classification
  • You have more than personal use amounts
NO DECLARATION NEEDED for:
  • Common OTC medications (Tylenol, Advil, antihistamines)
  • Most antibiotics
  • Birth control pills
  • Vitamins and supplements

Understanding the Difference: Import Certificate vs. Customs Declaration

📋 Import Certificate

Required BEFORE travel for:

  • More than 1 month supply of psychotropics
  • Any amount of certain narcotics
  • Injectable medications

Apply 4-6 weeks before travel

✍️ Customs Declaration

Required AT ARRIVAL for:

  • ANY controlled substance
  • Even if under 1 month supply
  • Even if you don't need a permit

Fill out on the plane/at airport

💊 Example: Methylphenidate (Ritalin/Concerta) - 12 pills for 12 days

📋
Import Certificate: NOT REQUIRED (under 30 days supply)
✍️
Customs Declaration: YES, REQUIRED (it's a psychotropic drug)
📄
What to bring: Original prescription + doctor's letter (just in case)

📝 How to Fill Out the Customs Declaration

1
On the "Prohibited Articles" section:

Check YES for "narcotics, psychotropics, firearms, swords"

2
On the reverse side:

Write: "[Medication name] - [amount] - personal medical use with prescription"

Example:
"Methylphenidate - 12 tablets - personal ADHD treatment with prescription"
3
At customs:
• Go to the RED channel (goods to declare)
• Show your prescription and medication
• Most officers will wave you through quickly
4
Keep ready:
• Medication in original bottle
• Prescription with your name
• Doctor's letter (if you have one)

✅ Pro Tip: Japanese customs officers appreciate honesty. Declaring shows you're following the rules. NOT declaring a controlled substance is risky - it could be seen as smuggling attempt.

📋 Common Medications - Declaration Required?

✅ MUST Declare:
  • Ritalin/Concerta (methylphenidate)
  • Adderall (if somehow bringing with permit)
  • Xanax (alprazolam)
  • Valium (diazepam)
  • Ativan (lorazepam)
  • Codeine combinations
  • Tramadol
  • Any sleeping pills
❌ No Declaration Needed:
  • Ibuprofen (Advil)
  • Acetaminophen (Tylenol)
  • Aspirin
  • Antihistamines (Claritin, Zyrtec)
  • Birth control
  • Antibiotics (most)
  • Blood pressure meds
  • Diabetes meds (non-injectable)

When in doubt, DECLARE IT. It's better to declare unnecessarily than risk being accused of smuggling.
